

     SECTION 1.  Chapter 302A, Hawaii Revised
Statutes, is amended by adding a new section to part II, subpart C, to be
appropriately designated and to read as follows:

     "ยง302A-     Student bill of
rights.  Students enrolled in public elementary, secondary, and high
schools shall have the following rights:

     (1)  Students shall be entitled to a safe
and secure school;

     (2)  Students shall be entitled to
classrooms that:

         (A)  Do not exceed twenty-four
students;

         (B)  Have an air temperature between
sixty-eight and seventy-six degrees Fahrenheit;

         (C)  Contain at least one desk and one
chair ergonomically appropriate for each student;

     (3)  Students shall be entitled to proper
nutrition during lunch time on instruction days;

     (4)  Students shall be entitled to eat lunch
at a table while sitting on a chair;

     (5)  Students shall be entitled to an
appropriate number of sanitary restroom facilities;

     (6)  Students shall be entitled to access to
clean, filtered drinking water; 

     (7)  Students shall be entitled access to a
complete science laboratory;

     (8)  Students shall be entitled to access to
a library with at least     age-appropriate books per student;

     (9)  Students shall be entitled to quality,
innovative education that keeps pace with society's evolving needs, including access
to computers and other technology;

    (10)  Students shall be entitled to physical
education that teaches students the rules of common ball games and athletic
events; 

    (11)  Students shall be entitled to
educational programs responsive to their needs, including remedial and gifted
programs; 

    (12)  Students shall be entitled to an
educational system that supports high-quality teaching and emphasizes student
growth and achievement; and

    (13)  Each school year, students shall be
provided with a minimum of one hundred and eighty days of instruction."

     SECTION 2.  New statutory material is
underscored.

     SECTION 3.  This Act shall take effect upon its
approval and shall apply starting with the 2017-2018 school year.
