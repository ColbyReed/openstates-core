 1                                         A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
 2   AN ACT TO STRENGTHEN THE CRIMINAL LAWS REGARDING ARSON AND TO
 3       APPROPRIATE FUNDS TO THE CONFERENCE OF DISTRICT ATTORNEYS TO
 4       ESTABLISH AND SUPPORT A POSITION FOR A RESOURCE PROSECUTOR.
 5   The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
 6               SECTION 1. Article 15 of Chapter 14 of the General Statutes is amended by adding a
 7   new section to read:
 8   "§ 14-67.2. Burning caused during commission of another felony.
 9       If any person shall, during the commission of a felony, by means of fire or explosive,
10   knowingly damage or knowingly cause, aid, abet, advise, encourage, hire, counsel, or procure
11   another to damage any dwelling, structure, building, or conveyance referenced in this Article, the
12   person shall be punished as a Class D felon."
13               SECTION 2. G.S. 14-69.3 reads as rewritten:
14   "§ 14-69.3. Arson or other unlawful burning that results in serious injury to a firefighter or
15               firefighter, law enforcement officer, fire investigator, or emergency medical
16               technician.
17       (a)     The following definitions apply in this section:
18               (1)     Emergency medical technician. – The term includes an emergency medical
19                       technician, an emergency medical technician-intermediate, and an emergency
20                       medical technician-paramedic, as those terms are defined in G.S. 131E-155.
21               (2)     Fire investigator. – The term includes any person who, individually or as part of
22                       an investigative team, has the responsibility and authority to determine the
23                       origin, cause, or development of a fire or explosion.
24       (b)     A person is guilty of a Class E felony if the person commits a felony under Article 15
25   of Chapter 14 of the General Statutes and a firefighter firefighter, law enforcement officer, fire
26   investigator, or emergency medical technician suffers serious bodily injury while discharging or
27   attempting to discharge the firefighter's or emergency medical technician's official duties on the
28   property, or proximate to the property, that is the subject of the firefighter'sfirefighter's, law
29   enforcement officer's, fire investigator's, or emergency medical technician's discharge or attempt
30   to discharge his or her respective duties. As used in this section, the term "emergency medical
31   technician" includes an emergency medical technician, an emergency medical
32   technician-intermediate, and an emergency medical technician-paramedic, as those terms are
33   defined in G.S. 131E-155."
34               SECTION 3. There is appropriated from the General Fund to the Conference of
35   District Attorneys the sum of one hundred fifteen thousand six hundred three dollars ($115,603) in

 1   recurring funds and the sum of three thousand eight hundred sixty-five dollars ($3,865) in
 2   nonrecurring funds for the 2017-2018 fiscal year to establish and support a position for a resource
 3   prosecutor. The resource prosecutor will serve as a resource for prosecutors, law enforcement,
 4   firefighters, fire marshals, arson investigators, medical professionals, and other allied professionals
 5   in the State on arson and homicide cases and also develop and provide training programs for
 6   prosecutors, law enforcement officers, and other allied professionals that will improve the ability
 7   to effectively prosecute arson and homicide cases.
 8               SECTION 4. Sections 3 and 4 of this act become effective July 1, 2017. The
 9   remainder of this act becomes effective December 1, 2017, and applies to offenses committed on
10   or after that date.
