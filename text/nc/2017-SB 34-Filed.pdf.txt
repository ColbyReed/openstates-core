                             SENATE BILL DRS55005-ML-22F* (12/15)



     Short Title:   Eminent Domain Const. Amendment.                                        (Public)
     Sponsors:      Senator B. Jackson (Primary Sponsor).
     Referred to:


 1                                        A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
 2   AN ACT TO AMEND THE NORTH CAROLINA CONSTITUTION TO PROHIBIT
 3       CONDEMNATION OF PRIVATE PROPERTY EXCEPT FOR A PUBLIC USE AND TO
 4       PROVIDE FOR THE PAYMENT OF JUST COMPENSATION WITH THE RIGHT OF
 5       TRIAL BY JURY IN ALL CONDEMNATION CASES.
 6   The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
 7               SECTION 1. Article I of the North Carolina Constitution is amended by adding a new
 8   section to read:
 9   "Sec. 38. Eminent domain.
10       Private property shall not be taken by eminent domain except for a public use. Just
11   compensation shall be paid and shall be determined by a jury at the request of any party."
12               SECTION 2. The amendment set out in Section 1 of this act shall be submitted to the
13   qualified voters of the State at a statewide election to be conducted on November 6, 2018, which
14   election shall be conducted under the laws then governing elections in the State. The question to
15   be used in the voting systems and ballots shall be:
16                                        "[ ] FOR        [ ] AGAINST
17               Constitutional amendment to prohibit condemnation of private property except for a
18   public use and to provide for the payment of just compensation with right of trial by jury in all
19   condemnation cases."
20               SECTION 3. If a majority of votes cast on the question are in favor of the amendment
21   set out in Section 1 of this act, the State Board of Elections shall certify the amendment to the
22   Secretary of State. The Secretary of State shall enroll the amendment so certified among the
23   permanent records of that office. The amendment set out in Section 1 of this act becomes effective
24   upon certification and applies to takings after that date.
25               SECTION 4. This act is effective when it becomes law.
