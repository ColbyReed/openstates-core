 1                                  A BILL TO BE ENTITLED
 2   AN ACT TO PROVIDE FLEXIBILITY IN THE SCHOOL CALENDAR, TO FACILITATE
 3      COMPENSATION TO SCHOOL EMPLOYEES, AND TO PROVIDE RELIEF TO
 4      STUDENTS ENROLLED IN AN EDUCATOR PREPARATION PROGRAM TO
 5      ACCOMMODATE EXTRAORDINARY CIRCUMSTANCES DUE TO HURRICANE
 6      FLORENCE.
 7   The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:
 8
 9   PART I. FLEXIBILITY IN THE SCHOOL CALENDAR AND COMPENSATION TO
10   SCHOOL EMPLOYEES
11               SECTION 1.1. Calendar Flexibility. – Notwithstanding G.S. 115C-75.8(d)(9),
12   115C-84.2(a)(1), 115C-150.12, 115C-218.85(a)(1), 115C-238.53(d), 115C-238.66(1)d.,
13   116-239.8(b)(2)c., Section 6(e) of S.L. 2018-32, and any other provision of State law to the
14   contrary, if the governing body of a public school unit closed any school under its control due to
15   unusual and extraordinary inclement weather conditions related to Hurricane Florence, calendar
16   flexibility for instructional time missed due to Hurricane Florence during the months of
17   September 2018 and October 2018 shall be provided as follows:
18               (1)     If the school is located in a public school unit that is located in a county
19                       designated before, on, or after the effective date of this act under a major
20                       disaster declaration by the President of the United States under the Stafford
21                       Act (P.L. 93-288) as a result of Hurricane Florence, in the discretion of its
22                       governing body, the public school unit may (i) make up any number of the
23                       instructional days or equivalent hours missed, (ii) deem as completed any

 1                       number of the instructional days or equivalent hours missed up to a total of 20
 2                       days, or (iii) implement any combination of both of the above.
 3               (2)     If the school is located in a public school unit that is not located in a county
 4                       designated under a major disaster declaration by the President of the United
 5                       States under the Stafford Act (P.L. 93-288) as a result of Hurricane Florence,
 6                       the following shall apply:
 7                       a.      If the public school unit's scheduled calendar will already meet 185
 8                               instructional days or 1025 instructional hours, no additional days or
 9                               hours for that school are required to comply with G.S. 115C-84.2.
10                       b.      For all other schools, for days missed in excess of two days, in the
11                               discretion of its governing body, the public school unit may do either
12                               or a combination of the following:
13                               1.       Make up any number of the instructional days or equivalent
14                                        hours missed.
15                               2.       Deem as completed any number of the instructional days or
16                                        equivalent hours missed.
17               For the purposes of this act, "public school unit" is defined as a local school
18   administrative unit, regional school, innovative school, laboratory school, charter school, or
19   residential school for the deaf or the blind.
20               SECTION 1.2. Employee Compensation. – Except for in the case of a charter school,
21   all employees employed by a public school unit shall be deemed to have been employed for any
22   scheduled instructional days missed due to Hurricane Florence during the months of September
23   2018 and October 2018 that a public school unit has deemed completed and is not required to
24   make up. Employees shall be compensated in the same manner they would have had they worked
25   on the scheduled instructional days missed. The board of directors of a charter school may, but
26   is not required to, provide such compensation for its employees.
27               Of the funds allocated to the Department of Public Instruction from the Hurricane
28   Florence Disaster Recovery Fund, the sum of up to six million five hundred thousand dollars
29   ($6,500,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2018-2019 fiscal year shall be used to provide
30   compensation authorized by this section to public school unit employees of schools participating
31   in the National School Lunch Program or School Breakfast Program for scheduled instructional
32   days when compensation would have been provided by school meal receipts or by federal funds.
33   Employees compensated using funds described in this section shall be compensated in the same
34   manner they would have had they worked on the scheduled instructional days missed.
35               If the funds described by this section are insufficient to provide compensation
36   authorized by this section to public school unit employees in schools participating in the National
37   School Lunch Program or School Breakfast Program for scheduled instructional days when
38   compensation would have been provided by school meal receipts or by federal funds, the
39   Department of Public Instruction shall develop a uniform criteria to determine the comparative
40   economic need of public school units to which this section applies and shall ensure that priority
41   is given to public school units with greatest economic need when awarding available funds.
42               SECTION 1.3. Reporting Requirement. – The Superintendent of Public Instruction
43   shall report to the chairs of the House of Representatives Committee on Appropriations and the
44   Senate Appropriations/Base Budget Committee, the Joint Legislative Education Oversight
45   Committee, and the Fiscal Research Division of the General Assembly on the implementation of
46   this act by public school units by March 15, 2019, and shall also provide any additional reports
47   or information requested by the Fiscal Research Division. The report required by this section
48   shall include information on the following:
49               (1)     Any days missed due to Hurricane Florence before, on, or after the effective
50                       date of this act.

12   PART II. COMPLETION OF THE EPP CLINICAL INTERNSHIP REQUIREMENT
13   FOR CERTAIN STUDENTS IMPACTED BY HURRICANE FLORENCE
14               SECTION 2.1. Notwithstanding G.S. 115C-269.25(d)(1), a student who is enrolled
15   in an educator preparation program (EPP) that is assigned the status of initially authorized or
16   authorized by the State Board of Education pursuant to G.S. 115C-269.5 may have the clinical
17   internship requirement set forth in G.S. 115C-269.25(d)(1) deemed completed if the student
18   meets the following conditions:
19               (1)      The student meets at least one of the following:
20                        a.      Is enrolled in an EPP that is located in a county declared a major
21                                disaster by the President of the United States under the Stafford Act
22                                (P.L. 93-288) as a result of Hurricane Florence.
23                        b.      Has been placed with an elementary or secondary partner school, as
24                                defined in G.S. 115C-269.1(14), that is located in a county declared a
25                                major disaster by the President of the United States under the Stafford
26                                Act (P.L. 93-288) as a result of Hurricane Florence.
27               (2)      The student has completed as much time in a clinical internship as practicable
28                        during the 2018 fall academic semester.
29               (3)      The student cannot graduate in December 2018 from the EPP without the
30                        clinical internship requirement being deemed completed pursuant to this
31                        section.
32               SECTION 2.2. By February 1, 2019, any EPP with an enrolled student who has his
33   or her clinical internship requirement deemed completed pursuant to Section 2.1 of this act shall
34   report to the State Board of Education with the following information:
35               (1)      Number of students who had the clinical internship requirement deemed
36                        completed.
37               (2)      Reasons for a clinical internship requirement being deemed completed.
38               (3)      Amount of time completed for a clinical internship.
39               (4)      Any other information required by the State Board.
40               By March 15, 2019, the State Board of Education shall report to the Joint Legislative
41   Education Oversight Committee on the information reported to the State Board by EPPs under
42   this section.
43
44   PART III. EFFECTIVE DATE
45            SECTION 3. This act is effective when it becomes law.
