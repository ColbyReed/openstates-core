 9   PART I. FLEXIBILITY IN THE SCHOOL CALENDAR AND COMPENSATION TO
10   SCHOOL EMPLOYEES
11               SECTION 1.1. Calendar Flexibility. – Notwithstanding G.S. 115C-75.8(d)(9),
12   115C-84.2(a)(1), 115C-150.12, 115C-218.85(a)(1), 115C-238.53(d), 115C-238.66(1)d.,
13   116-239.8(b)(2)c., Section 6(e) of S.L. 2018-32, and any other provision of State law to the
14   contrary, if the governing body of a public school unit closed any school under its control due to
15   unusual and extraordinary inclement weather conditions related to Hurricane Florence, calendar
16   flexibility for instructional time missed due to Hurricane Florence during the months of
17   September 2018 and October 2018 shall be provided as follows:
18               (1)     If the school is located in a public school unit that is located in a county
19                       designated before, on, or after the effective date of this act under a major
20                       disaster declaration by the President of the United States under the Stafford
21                       Act (P.L. 93-288) as a result of Hurricane Florence, in the discretion of its
22                       governing body, the public school unit may (i) make up any number of the
23                       instructional days or equivalent hours missed, (ii) deem as completed any
24                       number of the instructional days or equivalent hours missed up to a total of 20
25                       days, or (iii) implement any combination of both of the above.
26               (2)     If the school is located in a public school unit that is not located in a county
27                       designated under a major disaster declaration by the President of the United
28                       States under the Stafford Act (P.L. 93-288) as a result of Hurricane Florence,
29                       the following shall apply:
30                       a.      If the public school unit's scheduled calendar will already meet 185
31                               instructional days or 1025 instructional hours, no additional days or
32                               hours for that school are required to comply with G.S. 115C-84.2.
33                       b.      For all other schools, for days missed in excess of two days, in the
34                               discretion of its governing body, the public school unit may do either
35                               or a combination of the following:

 1                              1.        Make up any number of the instructional days or equivalent
 2                                        hours missed.
 3                               2.       Deem as completed any number of the instructional days or

 5               For the purposes of this act, "public school unit" is defined as a local school
 6   administrative unit, regional school, innovative school, laboratory school, charter school, or
 7   residential school for the deaf or the blind.
 8               SECTION 1.2. Employee Compensation. – Except for in the case of a charter school,
 9   all employees employed by a public school unit shall be deemed to have been employed for any
10   scheduled instructional days missed due to Hurricane Florence during the months of September
11   2018 and October 2018 that a public school unit has deemed completed and is not required to
12   make up. Employees shall be compensated in the same manner they would have had they worked
13   on the scheduled instructional days missed. The board of directors of a charter school may, but
14   is not required to, provide such compensation for its employees.
15               Of the funds allocated to the Department of Public Instruction from the Hurricane
16   Florence Disaster Recovery Fund, the sum of up to six million five hundred thousand dollars
17   ($6,500,000) in nonrecurring funds for the 2018-2019 fiscal year shall be used to provide
18   compensation authorized by this section to public school unit employees of schools participating
19   in the National School Lunch Program or School Breakfast Program for scheduled instructional
20   days when compensation would have been provided by school meal receipts or by federal funds.
21   Employees compensated using funds described in this section shall be compensated in the same
22   manner they would have had they worked on the scheduled instructional days missed.
23               If the funds described by this section are insufficient to provide compensation
24   authorized by this section to public school unit employees in schools participating in the National
25   School Lunch Program or School Breakfast Program for scheduled instructional days when
26   compensation would have been provided by school meal receipts or by federal funds, the
27   Department of Public Instruction shall develop a uniform criteria to determine the comparative
28   economic need of public school units to which this section applies and shall ensure that priority
29   is given to public school units with greatest economic need when awarding available funds.
30               SECTION 1.3. Reporting Requirement. – The Superintendent of Public Instruction
31   shall report to the chairs of the House of Representatives Committee on Appropriations and the
32   Senate Appropriations/Base Budget Committee, the Joint Legislative Education Oversight
33   Committee, and the Fiscal Research Division of the General Assembly on the implementation of
34   this act by public school units by March 15, 2019, and shall also provide any additional reports
35   or information requested by the Fiscal Research Division. The report required by this section
36   shall include information on the following:
37               (1)     Any days missed due to Hurricane Florence before, on, or after the effective
38                       date of this act.
39               (2)     Of the days missed, any scheduled make up days due to Hurricane Florence
40                       before, on, or after the effective date of this act, and the dates of those make
41                       up days.
42               (3)     Of the days missed, any days and hours deemed completed by the public
43                       school unit as a result of this act.
44               (4)     Any compensation provided to employees pursuant to Section 1.2 of this act.
45               For each component of the report, separate information shall be included on any
46   individual schools within the public school unit whose information differs from the rest of the
47   unit as a whole. Public school units shall provide information on implementation of this act in
48   the form requested by the Superintendent of Public Instruction no later than February 15, 2019.

50   PART II. COMPLETION OF THE EPP CLINICAL INTERNSHIP REQUIREMENT
51   FOR CERTAIN STUDENTS IMPACTED BY HURRICANE FLORENCE

 1               SECTION 2.1. Notwithstanding G.S. 115C-269.25(d)(1), a student who is enrolled
 2   in an educator preparation program (EPP) that is assigned the status of initially authorized or
 3   authorized by the State Board of Education pursuant to G.S. 115C-269.5 may have the clinical
 4   internship requirement set forth in G.S. 115C-269.25(d)(1) deemed completed if the student
 5   meets the following conditions:
 6               (1)      The student meets at least one of the following:
 7                        a.      Is enrolled in an EPP that is located in a county declared a major
 8                                disaster by the President of the United States under the Stafford Act
 9                                (P.L. 93-288) as a result of Hurricane Florence.
10                        b.      Has been placed with an elementary or secondary partner school, as
11                                defined in G.S. 115C-269.1(14), that is located in a county declared a
12                                major disaster by the President of the United States under the Stafford
13                                Act (P.L. 93-288) as a result of Hurricane Florence.
14               (2)      The student has completed as much time in a clinical internship as practicable
15                        during the 2018 fall academic semester.
16               (3)      The student cannot graduate in December 2018 from the EPP without the
17                        clinical internship requirement being deemed completed pursuant to this
18                        section.
19               SECTION 2.2. By February 1, 2019, any EPP with an enrolled student who has his
20   or her clinical internship requirement deemed completed pursuant to Section 2.1 of this act shall
21   report to the State Board of Education with the following information:
22               (1)      Number of students who had the clinical internship requirement deemed
23                        completed.
24               (2)      Reasons for a clinical internship requirement being deemed completed.
25               (3)      Amount of time completed for a clinical internship.
26               (4)      Any other information required by the State Board.
27               By March 15, 2019, the State Board of Education shall report to the Joint Legislative
28   Education Oversight Committee on the information reported to the State Board by EPPs under
29   this section.
30
31   PART III. EFFECTIVE DATE
32            SECTION 3. This act is effective when it becomes law.
