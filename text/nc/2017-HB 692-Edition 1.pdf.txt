 9   "§ 90-730. Short title.
10       This Article may be cited as the "North Carolina Naturopathic Doctors Certification Act."
11   "§ 90-731. Intent; purpose.
12       (a)     Intent. – The General Assembly finds that a significant number of residents of the
13   State of North Carolina choose complementary and alternative health care and declares that
14   naturopathic medicine is a distinct health care profession that affects the public health, safety,
15   and welfare and provides for choices in health care. The General Assembly concludes that
16   certification is in the current interest of North Carolina citizens to aid in protecting citizens
17   from deception, fraud, and damage to their health status. Certification can provide a process in
18   which citizens may more confidently rely on the level of skill, education, and competency
19   possessed by certified persons.
20       (b)     Purpose. – The purpose of this Article is to provide standards for the certification of
21   naturopathic doctors desiring to practice naturopathic medicine in this State and to ensure the
22   maintenance of professional competence and acceptable standards of practice.

47   "§ 90-733. Practice of naturopathic medicine; scope of practice.
48       (a)    Practice of Naturopathic Medicine. – A naturopathic doctor is a certified health care
49   provider having the same responsibilities as other licensed, certified, or registered health care
50   providers regarding public health laws, reportable diseases and conditions, communicable
51   disease control and prevention, and the recording of vital statistics. In diagnosing and treating

 1   an individual, a naturopathic doctor may employ the following therapies, modalities,
 2   procedures, or remedies consistent with naturopathic education and training:
 3               (1)     Dispense, administer, order, and advise the use of natural remedies derived
 4                       from or substantially similar in molecular structure or function to natural
 5                       sources for preventive and therapeutic purposes, including food, extracts of
 6                       food, nutraceuticals, vitamins, minerals, amino acids, enzymes, botanicals
 7                       and their extracts, homeopathic remedies prepared according to the
 8                       Homeopathic Pharmacopoeia of the United States, and all dietary
 9                       supplements and nonprescription drugs as defined by the federal Food, Drug,
10                       and Cosmetic Act, 21 U.S.C. § 301, et seq.
11               (2)     Order and perform physical examinations and physiological function tests.
12               (3)     Order, perform, and interpret laboratory tests, including performing waived
13                       tests as defined by the United States Food and Drug Administration Clinical
14                       Laboratory Improvement Amendments of 1998 (CLIA), including obtaining
15                       specimens to assess and treat disease.
16               (4)     Order diagnostic imaging, including X-ray, MRI, CT scan, ultrasound,
17                       mammogram, and bone densitometry to be conducted and interpreted by an
18                       appropriately licensed, registered, or certified health care professional. This
19                       excludes electrocardiograms, echocardiograms, electroencephalograms, and
20                       nuclear imaging which should be ordered, conducted, and interpreted by a
21                       physician licensed under Article 1 of this Chapter.
22               (5)     Perform hot or cold hydrotherapy, naturopathic physical medicine,
23                       electromagnetic therapy, and therapeutic exercise.
24               (6)     Perform health education and health counseling, including dietary and
25                       lifestyle counseling.
26               (7)     Utilize routes of administration for substances, including oral, nasal,
27                       auricular, ocular, rectal, vaginal, and transdermal.
28               (8)     Perform care incidental to superficial lacerations and abrasions, including
29                       the application of topical and local anesthetics and antimicrobials.
30               (9)     Remove foreign bodies located in the superficial tissues.
31       (b)     Prohibitions. – A naturopathic doctor may practice only within the scope of practice
32   authorized in subsection (a) of this section. A naturopathic doctor may not practice as any other
33   health care professional licensed, certified, or registered under another Article of this Chapter
34   unless otherwise licensed, certified, or registered by this State to do so. A naturopathic doctor
35   may not perform any of the following functions unless otherwise licensed by this State to do so:
36               (1)     Prescribe, dispense, or administer any legend drug, except as authorized by
37                       this Article.
38               (2)     Use general or spinal anesthetics.
39               (3)     Perform surgical procedures using a laser device.
40               (4)     Perform surgical procedures beyond superficial tissue.
41               (5)     Administer ionizing radioactive substances for therapeutic purposes.
42   "§ 90-734. Certification required; exemptions.
43       (a)     Certification Required. – No person shall practice, attempt to practice, or claim to
44   practice naturopathic medicine unless the person has been certified under the provisions of this
45   Article.
46       (b)     Use of Titles. – Only persons certified under this Article may use the titles
47   "Naturopathic Doctor," "Doctor of Naturopathic Medicine," "Doctor of Naturopathy,"
48   "Naturopathic Medicine," "Naturopath," "Certified Naturopathic Doctor," or the abbreviations
49   "N.D.," "ND," or any other titles, words, letters, abbreviations, or insignia indicating or
50   implying that the individual is a certified naturopathic doctor unless the individual has been
51   certified as a naturopathic doctor under this Article.

 1       (a)     Certification. – Upon application to the Board and payment of the required fees, an
 2   applicant may be certified under this Article as a naturopathic doctor if the applicant meets all
 3   of the following qualifications and conditions:
 4               (1)      Is of good moral and ethical character.
 5               (2)      Is a graduate of an approved program of naturopathic medicine as defined in
 6                        G.S. 90-732.
 7               (3)      Meets one, or both, of the following two conditions:
 8                        a.      Has successfully passed the Naturopathic Physicians Licensing
 9                                Examination (NPLEX), a competency-based national naturopathic
10                                licensing examination administered by the North American Board of
11                                Naturopathic Examiners or an equivalent successor agency
12                                recognized by the Board.
13                        b.      Has successfully passed a competency-based state or Canadian
14                                province naturopathic licensing examination administered prior to
15                                the existence of NPLEX and approved by the Board.
16               (4)      Provides the Board with a list of physicians licensed to practice medicine in
17                        this State who have agreed to consult with the applicant and accept referrals
18                        from the applicant. The applicant shall also provide the Board with letters of
19                        verification from the listed physicians. The list must include physicians with
20                        specialties in at least four of the following areas: allergy and immunology,
21                        cancer and oncology, cardiology, endocrinology and metabolism, family
22                        medicine, gastroenterology, internal medicine, obstetrics and gynecology,
23                        pediatrics, psychiatry, and urology.
24               (5)      Submits any other documentation the Board deems necessary to determine
25                        the applicant's fitness for certification under this Article. This documentation
26                        may include successful completion of a Board-approved jurisprudence
27                        examination on State laws and rules related to naturopathic medicine.
28       (b)     Renewal. – A certification expires two years after the date it is issued unless it is
29   renewed. To renew a certification, a licensee shall meet all of the following conditions:
30               (1)      Submit an application for certification renewal.
31               (2)      Pay the required fees.
32               (3)      Complete at least 40 hours of continuing education approved by the Board
33                        within the two-year period immediately prior to the renewal date.
34   Failure to renew a certification within six months of the date the certification expires shall
35   result in automatic forfeiture of the right to practice naturopathic medicine in this State until the
36   certification is reinstated.
37       (c)     Reinstatement. – A certified naturopathic doctor who has allowed the certification to
38   lapse by failure to renew within the time allowed under subsection (b) of this section may apply
39   for reinstatement. The Board may reinstate the applicant's certification if the applicant pays the
40   required fees, furnishes a statement of the reason for failure to apply for renewal before the
41   deadline, and complies with any other requirements established in rules adopted by the Board.
42   If the certification has lapsed for five years or longer, the Board may require the applicant to
43   satisfactorily complete one or more skills assessment or remediation courses approved by the
44   Board or to provide proof of active licensure, certification, or registration within the past five
45   years in another state, district, territory, or Canadian province.

48   resides in this State and has been licensed, certified, or registered to practice as a naturopathic
49   doctor in another state, district, territory, or Canadian province if both of the following

18   "§ 90-740. Disciplinary authority.
19        (a)     Authority. – The Board may impose probationary conditions upon a certified
20   naturopathic doctor, or it may deny, suspend, revoke, or refuse to issue or renew a certification
21   if the certified naturopathic doctor or applicant does any of the following:
22                (1)     Engages in any act or practice in violation of any of the provisions of this
23                        Article or of any of the rules adopted by the Board or aids, abets, or assists
24                        any other person in the violation of the provisions of this Article or rules
25                        adopted by the Board.
26                (2)     Gives false information to or withholds information from the Board in
27                        procuring or attempting to procure certification.
28                (3)     Has been convicted of or pled guilty or no contest to a crime that indicates
29                        that the person is unfit or incompetent to practice as a naturopathic doctor or
30                        that indicates the person has deceived or defrauded the public. A felony
31                        conviction shall result in the automatic revocation of certification by the
32                        Board unless the Board determines otherwise pursuant to rules adopted by
33                        the Board.
34                (4)     Has been declared mentally incompetent by a court of competent
35                        jurisdiction.
36                (5)     Habitually uses or is addicted to drugs or intoxicating liquors to the extent
37                        that it affects the certified naturopathic doctor's professional competency. If
38                        a certified naturopathic doctor violates this subdivision, the Board may
39                        require the certified naturopathic doctor to undergo a mental or physical
40                        examination by physicians designated by the Board before or after the
41                        certified naturopathic doctor has been charged. The results of the
42                        examination shall be admissible as evidence in a hearing before the Board.
43                (6)     Has demonstrated gross negligence, incompetency, or misconduct in the
44                        performance of naturopathic medical treatment.
45                (7)     Has had a health care provider license, certification, or registration denied,
46                        restricted, revoked, or suspended by another state, district, territory, or
47                        province.
48                (8)     Fails to consent to a criminal history record check.
49                (9)     Fails to respond, within a reasonable time, to inquiries from the Board
50                        concerning any matter affecting the individual's certification to practice
51                        naturopathic medicine.

31   "§ 90-741. Criminal history record check of applicants and certified naturopathic
32               doctors.
33       (a)     Criminal History Record Check. – The Board shall require a criminal history record
34   check for a person who is either certified as a naturopathic doctor under this Article or applying
35   for certification as a naturopathic doctor under this Article. The Board is responsible for
36   providing to the North Carolina Department of Justice the fingerprints of the person to be
37   checked, a form signed by the person consenting to the criminal record check and the use of
38   fingerprints and other identifying information required by the State or national repositories, and
39   any additional information required by the Department of Justice. The Board shall keep all
40   information obtained pursuant to this section confidential.
41       (b)     Conviction. – If a criminal history record check reveals one or more convictions, the
42   conviction does not automatically bar certification. The Board shall consider all of the
43   following factors regarding the conviction:
44               (1)     The level of seriousness of the crime.
45               (2)     The date of the crime.
46               (3)     The age of the person at the time of the conviction.
47               (4)     The circumstances surrounding the commission of the crime, if known.
48               (5)     The nexus between the criminal conduct of the person and the job duties of
49                       the position to be filled.
50               (6)     The person's prison, jail, probation, parole, rehabilitation, and employment
51                       records since the date the crime was committed.

 9   "§ 90-742. Reports; immunity from suit.
10       (a)     Report. – A person who has reasonable cause to suspect misconduct or incapacity of
11   a certified naturopathic doctor, or who has reasonable cause to suspect that a person is in
12   violation of this Article, may report the relevant facts to the Board. Upon receipt of a charge, or
13   upon its own initiative, the Board may give notice of an administrative hearing or may, after
14   diligent investigation, dismiss unfounded charges. A person who, in good faith, makes a report
15   pursuant to this section is immune from any criminal prosecution or civil liability resulting
16   from making the report.
17       (b)     Limited Immunity. – The Board, its officers, employees, and staff are immune from
18   any criminal prosecution or civil liability for exercising, in good faith, the powers and duties
19   given to the Board under this Article.
20   "§ 90-743. Third-party reimbursement.
21       Nothing in this Article shall be construed to require direct third-party reimbursement to
22   persons certified under this Article.
23   "§ 90-744. Advisory Council.
24       (a)     Created. – An Advisory Council is created to advise the Board and to develop
25   recommendations to foster coordination and collaboration between naturopathic doctors,
26   medical doctors, and other health care professionals for the purpose of providing appropriate
27   care for patients. The Council shall meet periodically and report its recommendations to the
28   Board and to the boards of directors for the North Carolina Medical Society and the North
29   Carolina Association of Naturopathic Physicians.
30       (b)     Membership. – The Advisory Council shall consist of the following six members:
31               (1)     Two licensed physicians appointed by the North Carolina Medical Society.
