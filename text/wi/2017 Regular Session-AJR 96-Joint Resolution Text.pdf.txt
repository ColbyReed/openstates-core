- 2018 LEGISLATURE
ASSEMBLY JOINT RESOLUTION 96
Relating to: recognizing January as Glaucoma Awareness Month in Wisconsin.
Whereas, glaucoma is a group of eye diseases that cause vision loss through
damage to the optic nerve; and
Whereas, over three million Americans have glaucoma, with experts
estimating that at least half of those people are not aware of it; and
Whereas, glaucoma is the world's second-leading cause of blindness according
the World Health Organization; and
Whereas, glaucoma has virtually no symptoms, is gradual, and causes
irreversible blindness; and
Whereas, glaucoma can be detected through comprehensive eye examinations,
and medications or surgery can help slow or prevent further vision loss; and
Whereas, glaucoma is the leading cause of blindness for African-Americans,
and high-risk groups include people over 60, diabetics, and people of African,
Hispanic, and Asian descent; and

Whereas, early detection is crucial to treating the condition where up to 40
percent of vision loss can occur without a person noticing; now, therefore, be it
Resolved by the assembly, the senate concurring, That the legislature
recognizes January as Glaucoma Awareness Month in Wisconsin.
