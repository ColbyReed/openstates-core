AN ACT
RELATING TO TAXATION; AMENDING CHAPTER 6, TITLE 63, IDAHO CODE, BY THE AD-
DITION OF A NEW SECTION 63-602FF, IDAHO CODE, TO AUTHORIZE A BOARD OF
COUNTY COMMISSIONERS TO GRANT A PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTION FOR PERSONAL
PROPERTY, TO PROVIDE PROCEDURES AND TO PROVIDE THAT THE LEGISLATURE
DECLARES THIS EXEMPTION NECESSARY AND JUST; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE
DATE.
Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
SECTION 1. That Chapter 6, Title 63, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
ignated as Section 63-602FF, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
63-602FF. PROPERTY EXEMPT FROM TAXATION -- PERSONAL PROPERTY --
COUNTY OPTION. (1) Beginning on January 1, 2019, a board of county commis-
sioners may grant a personal property tax exemption for all or a portion of
the market value of the personal property described in section 63-602KK,
Idaho Code, that is located in the county. The exemption shall be valid for
the exemption time period granted by the board of county commissioners.
(2) Before granting a property tax exemption under this section, the
board of county commissioners shall hold a public meeting regarding whether
to grant the exemption. The board of county commissioners shall provide
a summary of the application under consideration, a written notice of the
time, date and location of the public meeting, and an invitation to par-
ticipate in the meeting to all affected taxing districts and urban renewal
agencies at least five (5) calendar days before the meeting.
(3) Once an exemption has been granted, no further exemption approvals
are necessary as long as the requirements of this section have been met and
said property will no longer be assessed. Any portion of operating property
that is personal property as reported in section 63-602KK(8)(a)(ii), Idaho
Code, within the boundaries of the county granting an exemption under sub-
section (1) of this section shall also no longer be assessed by the state tax
commission.
(4) The legislature declares this exemption necessary and just.
(5) No later than the third Monday of November of each year, the county
clerk of each county shall certify to the state tax commission the amount of
new exemptions from property taxes under this section in that county for that
year. The certification shall identify the property receiving tax reduc-
tions, the value of the property, the property's location, the amount of the
tax levy applicable to personal property in the location, and the tax before
and after the exemption allowed in this section. The certification shall be
in the form prescribed by the state tax commission and shall include such ad-
ditional information as the commission may require by rule as needed to im-
plement the purpose of this section. The certification shall be reviewed
and, if necessary, corrected by the state tax commission. Any personal prop-
erty that has been granted a permanent exemption by the board of county com-
missioners may be removed from the county assessor's property roll following
the certification.
SECTION 2. This act shall be in full force and effect on and after Jan-
uary 1, 2019.
